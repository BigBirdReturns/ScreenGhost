# Original premise ledger

The experiment exists to test one claim:

> A phone is a constrained, recurring utility. ScreenGhost may pay a high cost to understand an unfamiliar surface once, but later operation should become cheaper, calmer, teacher-blind, and transferable across applications that share the same interface grammar.

The campaign therefore does not treat a successful screenshot parser as the conclusion. It measures the complete cold-to-warm change.

| Dimension | Cold teaching | Warm execution | Required conclusion |
|---|---|---|---|
| Privileged structure | available to teacher | unavailable to action policy | zero warm teacher dependencies |
| Large VLM | permitted and accounted | exception only | at least one complete warm task with zero calls |
| Visual recognition | lesson compilation | visual index and prototypes | known screens recovered from pixels |
| Planning | open-ended/oracle in emulator | graph, grammar, cache | known route reused |
| Motor behavior | externally receipted | single-flight | no duplicate or overlapping action |
| Waiting | explicit settlement | explicit settlement | visible postcondition before commit |
| Cross-app transfer | teacher creates generic grammar | untaught app uses grammar | at least one useful held-out action |
| Novelty | teacher knows truth | student must reject | zero confident unknown/look-alike matches |
| Cost | simulated or measured, labeled | simulated or measured, labeled | warm GPU-active cost at least 50% lower in emulator |

The physical machine is a final transport and performance receipt. It is not allowed to redefine the premise after the emulator result is known.
